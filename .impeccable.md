## Design Context

### Users
People tracking gym sessions from a phone-first interface, usually during or right after training. They need to log workouts quickly, monitor strength and body-weight trends, and review weekly consistency without getting buried in admin.

### Brand Personality
Focused, athletic, and confident. The interface should feel sharp and motivating, with data presented in a calm but high-performance way rather than playful or corporate.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark mobile-first fitness dashboard with dense but glanceable reporting, inspired by modern workout tracker apps. Visual direction should favor strong metric typography, compact panels, and restrained electric-blue highlights over decorative glass effects.

### Design Principles
1. Make key workout stats readable in under three seconds.
2. Keep primary actions thumb-friendly and obvious on mobile.
3. Use visual hierarchy to separate quick report views from deep history views.
4. Show progress with meaningful data visuals, not decorative charts.
5. Preserve the app's existing dark training-focused tone while avoiding generic AI dashboard patterns.
